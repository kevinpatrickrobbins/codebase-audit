---

## Live Discovery

- Fetch https://www.iso.org/standard/27001 — current standard version.
- Fetch https://www.iso.org/standard/27002 — companion 27002 (control implementation guidance).

The 2022 revision restructured Annex A from 114 controls in 14 clauses
to **93 controls in 4 themes** (organizational, people, physical,
technological).

---

## Scope

ISO 27001 certification often runs in parallel with SOC 2. The
fundamental difference: SOC 2 attests to controls operation; ISO 27001
attests to an Information Security Management System (ISMS) — a system
that *generates and maintains* controls.

The audit scope:
- **Core clauses (4–10):** ISMS requirements
- **Annex A:** 93 controls organized in 4 themes

---

## Step 1: ISMS scope statement

The team's ISMS scope statement defines what's in / out of scope.
Typically all customer-facing infrastructure + supporting business
operations. Out-of-scope often includes physical security (inherited from
cloud provider) and HR systems for very small teams.

Most of the ISMS scope is OUT_OF_BAND from a code audit standpoint
(policy, process, governance). What's auditable from code = subset of
Annex A technological controls.

## Severity calibration

ISO 27001 is more flexible than SOC 2 in that the team can mark a control
"not applicable" with documented justification. Severity:

- **Critical:** in-scope Annex A.8 control with GAP (technological
  controls are the auditable core).
- **High:** ISMS scope undefined; Statement of Applicability absent.
- **Medium:** OUT_OF_BAND policies that need to be drafted.
- **Low:** documentation refinements.

For projects pursuing both SOC 2 and ISO 27001, many controls overlap —
the cross-reference report should highlight where evidence does
double-duty.
